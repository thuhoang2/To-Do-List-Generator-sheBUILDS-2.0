Guide

If you would like to follow a tutorial on how to craft an effective to do list, please refer to this guide! I will guide you step-by-step on how to fill in your to do list. To get started, simply flip to the next page using the 'Step 1' button.

If you don't need a short tutorial, feel free to hide this guide using the 'Hide Guide' button below.

*Caution: Once the guide is hidden, you will have to reload your page to access it again.

1. Deciding a focus

How do you plan to use this to do list? Will it be a daily to do list or an action plan for a specific project? Determine the focus of your list and change the title on the left.

Once finished, continue flipping to the subsequent pages until you hit the last one. Have fun crafting your to do list!

*Caution: Every time you edit the left-side list, this guide will switch back to its welcome page. You will have to open your according step again.

2. Choosing your tasks

Now with your focus, think about the tasks that you need to complete. Make sure not to overload yourself with unnecessary tasks and that you actually plan to complete your to-dos.

Set a goal for yourself. How productive do you want to be? How much work would you like to handle? Having a clear expectation will help you be more mindful of completing your plan.

Reflect on your usual performance too. How productive can you 'realistically' be? Do you always plan 6 tasks for a day but only end up achieving 4 of them? Being aware of your actual performance will help you decide the right tasks to do.

3. Leading a life of balance

Does any of you task look too vague or big? This kind of task can be intimidating, and you may not have a clear direction on how to handle it. An useful tip is to break them down. Instead of putting something broad like "prepare for the party", break it down into smaller tasks like "buy drinks and snacks" and "decorate the house with balloons".

Do every task on your list look huge and overwhelming? It helps to add in a few simple tasks like "clean my room" or "shower". Finishing these easily-crossed-out tasks can make you feel extra-productive!

4. Prioritizing is key

Not every task holds the same level of importance. Decide on your most urgent task - something that is both important and needed to be completed right now - and place these tasks at the top of your list. Now you know where to focus most of your energy.

5. Some final trimming

Instead of thinking just about "what to do", consider the "when" and "where" as well. For each task that you have come up with, add a few words to specify a when and where you would like to complete this task.

You can also add in any extra detail that you find necessary, such as a phone number/email for your "contact my professor to ask about my report" task, for example.

6. Don't forget about motivation!

*This is an extra step, you may want to skip this if all you need is just a to do list.

Do you notice that below your to do list is a 'Motivation' section? Here, you can put in anything that can help motivate you. Maybe a reminder on why you are doing this, or some inspirational quotes you found on the Internet (I already put in one for you!)

You can plan a reward system too. Maybe you will get yourself some ice cream if you finish half of your tasks, then allow yourself to watch 2 K-Drama episodes once everything is finished. These rewards can be anything, as long as they can motivate you to get tasks done.

Done!

Phew, what a long journey trying to craft a perfect to do list. The final step is to look at your list one last time to see whether you missed any task or whether you can delete/specify some of them.

If you believe that your version is set to go, then congrats! I wish you the best of luck in completing everything you've planned for yourself.
